Vietnam, the home of the food

What to know about vietnam

It is one of the most popular countries in south east Asia visited by the backpackers across the world, but it still isn’t the country that people go visiting back like its neighbor Thailand, which converts many of its visitors to permanent fans that end up going there back and forth. While Vietnam clicks with most people, it’s a shame that still many aren’t aware how gorgeous this country is! Maybe they didn’t know a few things or they hadn’t prepared or planned well. Whatever the reason is, knowing a few things before will give you an idea of what to expect in Vietnam, making you like and enjoy it when you visit. So go ahead and check out these things to know before going to Vietnam.

their Specialities

Vietnamese food is distinct and unforgettable. The cuisine relies on a balance of salty, sweet, sour and hot flavours, achieved through use of nuoc mam, a fermented fish sauce, cane sugar, the juice of kalamansi citrus fruit or tamarind and chilli peppers. Dishes use plenty of fresh herbs but tend not to be overly spicy, as chilli sauces are served separately. We've picked 3 essentials Vietnamese foods everyone shoud try.

The cha gio, also named "nem" in France

It is composed of ground meat, usually pork, wrapped in rice paper and deep-fried.

The pho

Phở is a Vietnamese soup consisting of broth, rice noodles, herbs, and meat.

A must try, the Bo bun

The bò bún is a type of Vietnamese salad of rice vermicelli served cold or hot, garnished and sprinkled with nuoc-mâm sauce.

the politic

Vietnam is a one-party socialist republic. The current Vietnamese state traces its direct lineage back to the Democratic Republic of Vietnam (North Vietnam) and the 1945 August Revolution led by Hồ Chí Minh.

Nguyễn Phú Trọng, the current president

He is a Vietnamese politician who is the current General Secretary of the Communist Party of Vietnam since 2011.

Vietnam communist protestation

The communist politic in Vietnam maintains a unitary government and has centralised control over the state, military and media.

A powerful army

The People's Army of Vietnam, also known as the Vietnamese People's Army (VPA), is the military force of the Socialist Republic of Vietnam.

places to visit

Vietnam is an astonishing mix of natural highlights and cultural diversity. The scenery ranges from jagged peaks seen from winding mountain passes down to verdant paddy fields painted every shade of green in the palette, while the nation's long history and amazing number of ethnic minorities mean that culture vultures will find plenty to admire.

The Vietnam Mekong river

It is the world's twelfth longest river and the seventh longest in Asia. This river is a major trade route between western China and Southeast Asia.

A dragon legacy, the Halong Bay

Hạ Long Bay, in northeast Vietnam, is known for its emerald waters and thousands of towering limestone islands topped by rainforests.

The Golden Bridge of Da Nang

The Golden Bridge is a 150 meter long pedestrian bridge in the Bà Nà Hills resort, near Da Nang.
