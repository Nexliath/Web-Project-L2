What to know about china

After several long Dynasties of conservative emperors, China started to develop and change. Nowadays, being tourist-oriented, it attracts more and more people tha come visit every year. I has many things to offer: impressive architecture, spectacular natural wonders, exotic culture and rich history.

their Specialities

With a long history, unique features, numerous styles and exquisite cooking, Chinese cuisine is one important constituent part of Chinese culture. Chinese traditional dishes are famous for color, aroma, taste, meaning and appearance.

Hot pot soup

A Chinese cooking method, prepared with a simmering pot of soup stock at the dining table, containing a variety of Asian ingredients.

Chinese dumplings

Chinese dish that consists of pieces of dough wrapped around a filling, or of dough with no filling.

The famous noodles

Chinese noodles vary widely according to the region of production, ingredients and manner of preparation.

the politic

The politics of the People's Republic of China takes place in a framework of a socialist republic run by a single party, the Communist Party of China, headed by the General Secretary. State power within the People's Republic of China (PRC) is exercised through the Communist Party, the Central People's Government (State Council) and their provincial and local representation.

The president, Xi Jinping

General Secretary of the Communist Party of China and Chairman of the Central Military Commission since 2013.

A communist country

The Communist Party of China is the founding and ruling political party of the People's Republic of China and the second largest political party in the world.

A military power

The People's Liberation Army (PLA) is the armed forces of the People's Republic of China. It is the world's third-most powerful military.

places to visit

China is huge, and diverse in landscape and culture. We have selected China’s most popular places for tourism, to help you narrow down your choices and maximize your time discovering the highlights of China.

The forbidden city

The Forbidden City is a palace complex in central Beijing, China. It houses the Palace Museum, and was the former Chinese imperial palace of the Emperor of China from the Ming dynasty to the end of the Qing dynasty, between 1420 and 1924.

One of the 7 wonders, the Great Wall

The Great Wall of China is the collective name of a series of fortification systems generally built across the historical northern borders of China to protect territories of empires against various nomadic groups of the steppe and their polities.

The terracota army

The Terracotta Army is a collection of terracotta sculptures depicting the armies of Qin Shi Huang, the first Emperor of China. It is a form of funerary art buried with the emperor in 210–209 BCE with the purpose of protecting the emperor in his afterlife.
